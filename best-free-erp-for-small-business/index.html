Read the fine print

What "free ERP" can quietly hide

Three boundaries to check in any system before you commit your data and processes.

GATE

Feature-gated editions

The free edition exists, but reports, mobile apps or key modules sit in a paid tier. Compare edition pages, not marketing pages.

SEAT

Per-user pricing later

Free for a few seats, then every new employee raises the bill. Prefer licenses where team growth costs nothing.

LOCK

Hosting lock-in

If the free plan only exists in the vendor's cloud, moving out later is a migration project. Self-hosting keeps the exit door open.

Common questions

Free ERP, without the fine print

Something unanswered? Ask us directly.

Is free ERP software really free?

The license can be genuinely free — every system here is open source. You still invest in hosting (a small server or VPS) and implementation time. The differences are in what each edition includes: some projects keep every module free, others reserve advanced features for paid editions.

Do I need my own server?

For the self-hosted route, yes — but a modest one. MyCompany starts on about 4 GB of RAM. Most projects here also have managed cloud options from the vendor or third-party providers if you prefer not to run a server.

Which free ERP is the easiest to start with?

Dolibarr is famously easy to launch on inexpensive PHP hosting. MyCompany installs on a clean Linux server or Docker in roughly 15 minutes. ERPNext and Odoo are straightforward via their images, though their stacks are heavier to maintain long-term.

What is the catch with Odoo's free options?

Odoo Community is genuinely open source, and Odoo advertises a One App Free online plan. But many well-known features belong to the paid Enterprise edition, and multi-app online usage is subscription-based. Review Odoo's official editions and pricing pages for the current boundaries.

Why is MyCompany listed first?

Because it is our product, and this page says so openly instead of pretending to be neutral. Every entry lists honest strengths and trade-offs, each system has a free demo or download — so the fairest test is running your own workflow in each candidate.
