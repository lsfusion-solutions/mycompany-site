Open-source ERP for small manufacturing

MyCompany is a free, open-source manufacturing ERP for small workshops and production businesses. It covers the production cycle end to end: bills of materials, manufacturing orders, material reservation, work centers and actual-cost calculation — all inside one system that also handles sales, purchasing, inventory and invoicing.

Unlike closed manufacturing ERP suites priced per user per month, MyCompany is released under the Apache 2.0 license: no license fees, no hidden costs and no vendor lock-in. It is built on the lsFusion platform (LGPL v3, Java and PostgreSQL, fully ACID) and can be installed on your own server in 10-15 minutes — your production data stays with you.

If you are comparing open source manufacturing software, the fastest way to evaluate MyCompany is the live demo — no registration required.

Bills of materials

The built-in BOM software defines what each product is made of and how it is made:

  • Components with quantities per base quantity, so material needs scale proportionally with the ordered amount
  • By-products that come out of the process alongside the main product
  • Operations with assigned work centers, start times and durations
  • A default BOM that is selected automatically when you create an order
  • Nested BOMs for multi-level, hierarchical product structures

Manufacturing orders and material reservation

A manufacturing order plans either production or disassembly. The to-produce quantity drives the material requirements, and before the order starts the system checks stock availability and reserves the materials it needs. For make-to-order production, manufacturing orders are created directly from sales orders, so customer demand flows straight to the shop floor.

Purchasing connected to production

When materials are missing, purchase orders are created straight from the manufacturing order list — aggregated across orders and grouped by vendor. "Auto order" suggestions combine sales forecasts with production needs, so replenishment reflects what you actually plan to build.

Work centers and capacity planning

Work centers model your machines and production areas. Each operation becomes a work order that moves through Ready, In Progress and Done, with bulk actions for processing many at once. The work center load dashboard shows a capacity matrix by day, and new work orders can be created directly from its cells — simple, visual production planning without a separate scheduling tool.

Costing on actual figures

Instead of relying on standard costs, MyCompany calculates what an order really cost: material write-offs plus scrap, labor taken from project time entries, and service costs from supplier bills distributed to the order. Unit cost is the line cost divided by the actual quantity produced, and cost ledger entries are posted when the order is done.

Part of a complete, customizable ERP

Manufacturing works hand in hand with the sales, purchase, inventory and invoicing modules, including lot and serial-number tracking from inventory. You can also unbuild products back into components, record production scrap and print lot labels. Because the source code is open and the logic is written in lsFusion modules, you can adapt forms, fields and workflows to your own processes.

FAQ

Is MyCompany suitable for a small manufacturer?

Yes — that is exactly who it is built for. It is free, installs on your own server in 10-15 minutes, and covers BOMs, orders, work centers and costing without the overhead of a heavyweight ERP project.

Does it handle multi-level bills of materials?

Yes. BOMs can be nested to describe hierarchical product structures, with component quantities defined per base quantity so they scale with the order.

How is the cost of a manufactured product calculated?

On actuals: material write-offs and scrap, labor from project time entries and services from supplier bills are distributed to the order. Unit cost equals the line cost divided by the actual quantity, posted to the cost ledger when the order is done.

Can I plan production by work center capacity?

Yes. The work center load dashboard shows capacity as a matrix by day, and you can create work orders directly from its cells.

Does it support make-to-order production?

Yes. Manufacturing orders can be created straight from sales orders, and missing materials can be purchased from the same order list, grouped by vendor.

Is it really free?

Yes. MyCompany is open source under the Apache 2.0 license and is self-hosted, so there are no per-user fees, hidden costs or license lock-in. Paid help with customization and support is optional.
