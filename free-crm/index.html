Free open-source CRM for small and medium businesses

MyCompany is a free open-source CRM under the Apache 2.0 license, built with small businesses in mind. There are no license fees and no per-user charges: you install the system on your own server in 10–15 minutes and keep full control over your customer data.

The CRM covers the whole sales funnel: leads with statuses, priorities and revenue forecasts, a visual Lead board (kanban), phone calls and emails attached to each lead, and sales reports. And because this CRM is part of the MyCompany ERP, a lead turns into an order and an invoice in one click — no retyping data into another system. Everything runs on the lsFusion platform (LGPL v3, Java + PostgreSQL, ACID transactions).

Leads with full sales context

Every lead carries a status, a type, a color-coded priority, tags (say, "trade fair" or "follow-up"), an assigned sales person and contact details. The Expected revenue, Probability and Expected closing fields take care of forecasting. You narrow the lead list down with the Opened, Closed and My leads views — or filter by type, sales person, item, campaign, medium and source — and the "Create Legal entity" action turns lead data into a customer record without retyping.

Lead board (kanban)

The Lead board (kanban) shows your funnel visually: columns match lead statuses, and moving a deal to the next stage is a simple drag of a card. The order of cards is remembered separately for each user, so every sales person arranges the board the way that suits them.

Calls and emails attached to the lead

Communication history should not live in personal inboxes. A registered phone call becomes a new lead in one action — the phone number is carried over automatically — or gets attached to an existing lead. Emails are sent straight from the lead card, so the entire conversation with a customer stays in one place.

From lead to order and invoice

When a deal goes through, nothing is retyped into another program. Once document types are set in the CRM settings, a sales order or an invoice is created straight from the lead: the customer, delivery address and expected revenue carry over, and the created document keeps its link to the lead. The CRM works in one database with inventory, sales and invoicing — it is part of a full ERP, not a separate tool.

Lead report and lost-deal analysis

The Lead report is a pivot table you can slice by status, sales person, priority, tags, revenue, probability, campaign, medium and source — and over time. Closing a lead as Lost requires picking a lost reason, so you know not only how many deals you lose, but why.

Zadarma IP telephony

The Zadarma integration logs calls automatically, recognizes customers by their phone number and offers click-to-call. Call recordings can be played back from the system, and the WebRTC widget lets you take calls right in the browser.

FAQ

Is this CRM really free?

Yes. MyCompany is open source under the Apache 2.0 license — no license fees and no user limits. You install it on your own server, and your customer data stays with you. The full source code is on GitHub.

Is there a kanban board?

Yes — the Lead board (kanban), where columns match statuses and you change a lead's status by dragging its card. The card order is remembered separately for each user.

Does the CRM support telephony?

Yes, through the Zadarma integration: automatic call logging, customer recognition by phone number, click-to-call, call recordings and a WebRTC widget in the browser.

How does a lead become an order or invoice?

Point the CRM settings at your document types, then create a sales order or an invoice straight from the lead card. The customer, delivery address and expected revenue carry over automatically, and the document keeps its link to the lead.

Is the CRM connected to inventory and invoicing?

Yes. The CRM is a module of the MyCompany ERP — leads, orders, invoices and stock levels live in one database, with no integrations between separate programs.

What are the technical requirements?

A server with 4 GB of RAM is enough. Installation is one line on Linux, an installer on Windows, or Docker, and updating means replacing a single JAR file. Documentation is available in English, Russian, and Polish.
