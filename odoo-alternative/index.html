MyCompany as an open-source Odoo alternative

Odoo is a mature and widely used ERP with a free open-source Community edition (LGPLv3) and a commercial Enterprise edition, and for many companies a solid choice. MyCompany takes a different approach: it is an open-source ERP for small and medium-sized businesses where every module is free under the Apache 2.0 license, built for self-hosting and deep customization on the lsFusion platform.

The two systems solve the same problem with different trade-offs. This page compares them honestly — licensing, pricing, hosting, customization, and upgrades — so you can pick the tool that fits your situation.

When MyCompany is a better fit

  • You want every module free under a single Apache 2.0 license — Master Data, Accounting, Inventory, Invoicing, Purchase, Manufacturing, Sales, POS, CRM, Projects, HR, and Transport — with no separate paid edition.
  • You want no per-user fees at any team size.
  • You plan to self-host on modest hardware: 4 GB of RAM is enough, with a one-line Linux install, a Windows installer, or Docker.
  • Your workflows are non-standard and you expect to adapt the data model, forms, and logic in the lsFusion language.
  • You want custom code that is designed to survive updates: modules live outside the product, so an update typically means replacing one JAR while your .lsf modules stay in place (changes in a new version may still require adjustments).

When Odoo may be the better choice

  • You want SaaS hosting without running your own server — that is what Odoo Online is for.
  • You need native mobile apps, which ship with Odoo Enterprise.
  • You want a much larger ecosystem: odoo.com claims 40,000+ community apps and 100,000+ developers, and its partner directory lists thousands of partners worldwide.
  • You need country-specific accounting localizations out of the box, which Enterprise Accounting provides for many countries.
  • You rely on an integrated marketing, website, and eCommerce suite.

Licensing and cost

MyCompany is simple: the entire product is Apache 2.0, free for commercial use, no editions, nothing held back.

Odoo Community is LGPLv3 and free, but a number of features are Enterprise-only under a proprietary per-user monthly subscription. Per odoo.com's official editions page (July 2026), these include full Accounting (Community has Invoicing only), Studio, native mobile apps, Payroll, inventory Barcode, manufacturing Shopfloor and Scheduling, Helpdesk, Field Service, and version upgrades, among others. There is also a free "One App Free" plan — one app, unlimited users, hosted on Odoo Online; see odoo.com/pricing.

Hosting and control

MyCompany is self-hosted only: you run it on your own server, a VPS, or your local network. There is no vendor SaaS — you (or a partner) host the system, and your data and upgrade schedule stay entirely under your control.

Odoo offers three hosting options: Odoo Online, a SaaS running the Enterprise edition only, where custom modules and third-party apps cannot be installed, per Odoo's docs; Odoo.sh, a PaaS that requires an Odoo subscription; and on-premise hosting for both editions.

Customization and upgrades

Odoo customizations are Python and XML modules on PostgreSQL. Odoo releases one major version per year, with standard support for the three most recent. Per Odoo's upgrade documentation, a database with custom modules cannot be upgraded until those modules are adapted to the target version, and keeping customizations compatible is the responsibility of their maintainer.

MyCompany customizations are written in the declarative lsFusion language — a single language for the data model, logic, and forms. Extension modules are plain files that live outside the product JAR, so a product update means replacing one JAR while your modules stay in place. In practice this can significantly reduce upgrade work — though a new product version may still require adjustments to custom modules, and updates should be tested before rollout.

Technology stack

Odoo runs its logic tier in Python on PostgreSQL, with a web client built on the OWL framework (TypeScript). MyCompany runs the lsFusion application server on the JVM (Java), also on PostgreSQL, and renders the web client natively. Transactions are ACID out of the box, and the lsFusion platform itself is open source under LGPL v3.

FAQ

Is MyCompany a drop-in replacement for Odoo?

No. It is a different platform with a different data model — an alternative to evaluate, not a compatible clone. Moving from Odoo means re-implementing your workflows and importing your data.

Is everything in MyCompany really free?

Yes. All modules ship under one Apache 2.0 license, with no paid edition, no per-user fees, and full source code on GitHub. Documentation is available in English, Russian, and Polish.

Can I migrate data from Odoo?

There is no ready-made migrator. Both systems store data in PostgreSQL, and MyCompany imports common formats — Excel, CSV, JSON, and XML — so master data and opening balances can be imported. Plan it as a project, not a button click.

Which is easier to run on a small server?

MyCompany needs about 4 GB of RAM and installs with one line on Linux, a Windows installer, or Docker. Odoo can also be run on-premise — and if you prefer not to run a server at all, there is Odoo Online.

Where can I try MyCompany?

A live demo runs at demo.lsfusion.org/mycompany — no installation or registration needed.
